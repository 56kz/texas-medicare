Medicare Supplement Plans

What is a private supplement or medigap plan?

A private supplement is plan obtained with a private insurance company that pays secondary to Medicare. With a private supplement Medicare is billed first, then the remaining amount is billed to the plan. This type of plan offers more flexibility in choosing providers, because there generally is no network that a member must use, instead they may go to any doctor that accepts Original Medicare.

What is the cost for a private supplement?

Private supplement can have an additional premium beyond the part B premium ranging from $80 to $170 dollars depending on the type of plan, service area, and company you choose.

Who can join a private supplement?

Generally anyone can join a private supplement within six months of starting Medicare without the risk of being denied coverage or paying a higher premium, because of pre-existing conditions. After the six month period there would be the possibility of full underwriting.

What type of coverage do you get with a private supplement?

There are different standardized plans that are labeled A thru N. The higher the premium on the plan, will generally purchase better coverage. Since these plans are standardized, the same coverage with a Plan A with one company would be equal to the coverage with a plan A with another company, the only difference is the price and stability of the company you choose.

Who is a private supplement recommended for?

A private supplement is a great fit for anyone who wants flexibility in choosing their own doctors, and can afford the additional monthly premium associated with this type of plan.

Medicare Advantage Plans

What is a Medicare Advantage (MA)?

A. Medicare Advantage, also known as a Part C, are private health plans offered through private health insurance companies. These private health insurance companies have a contract with Medicare to provide these benefits for individuals who choose to enroll in those plans. Once an individual joins a Medicare Advantage plan, that company now coordinates their A & B parts, instead of Medicare. All payments are made by that insurance company, not through Medicare directly.

What are the Advantages of an MA plan?

With an MA plan, the members cost sharing is generally lower than that with Original Medicare alone. Also, these plans offer a maximum out-of-pocket protection that is not offered by Original Medicare, than can save the member thousands in catastrophic costs.

How do you join a MA?

Anyone that has Medicare parts A & B, lives in the service area, and does not have end-stage renal disease can join an MA plan. Unlike a, Medicare Supplement anyone may join an MA, during certain times of the year, regardless of pre-existing health conditions.

What are the costs to join an MA plan?

To join an MA plan there can be additional costs associated beyond the $104.90 part B medicare premium. These additional premiums vary from $0 to over $100 depending on several factors, such as service areas, type of plan, and benefits provided.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I enroll in a Medicare Part C plan, Medicare supplement, or Medicare Advantage plan if I am not enrolled in Medicare?

No. You must be enrolled in Medicare in order to be eligible to enroll in a Medicare Part D plan, Medicare Supplement, or Medicare Advantage plan. A Medicare Part D prescription drug plan only requires that you be enrolled in Medicare Part A and B. However, in order to enroll within a Medicare Supplement plan or a Medicare Advantage plan, you must first be enrolled in both Medicare Part A and Medicare Part B.

All Medicare Part D plans cover the same medications?

No, each plan may have a unique list of covered drugs. The list of covered drugs is known as a formulary. Medicare requires all Medicare Part D plans to cover at least two medications in each therapeutic category/class approved by Medicare. The drugs within the formulary are assigned to tiers. The tier determines the co-payment or out-of-pocket costs a person within the plan will pay for the drug. If you take a medication that is not covered on your Part D plan's formulary, you will pay full retail price.

Are Medicare and Medicaid the same thing?

No. Medicare is a federal health coverage program designed for the elderly as well as individuals with certain qualifying health conditions such as End Stage Renal Disease. Medicaid is a state-run health coverage program primarily targeting low-income individuals within the state and kids.

If I enroll in a Medicare Advantage plan or a Medicare Supplement plan, do I still have to pay my Medicare Part B premium?

Yes. You will have to pay your monthly Medicare Part B premium as well as the monthly premium you pay towards your Medicare Supplement plan.

If I enroll in a Medicare Supplement plan, will it also cover my spouse?

No. You and your spouse must each enroll in a Medicare Supplement plan or Medicare Advantage Plan.

Do I have to be enrolled in Medicare Part B before I enroll in a Medicare Advantage program?

Yes.
